A Font Fiasco

kazooduck

Recently, I’ve become fascinated with font design, and have chosen “Roboto Slab” to be somewhat of my identity. Why? I believe fonts are a part of making a unique user-experience(UX) design, and if your design can stand out, your projects will with it. 

Welcome to my recent fascination with fonts, identity, sustainable design, and to combat aging trends.

Why Serif?

The choice between Serif and Sans

“[A serif is] A stroke added as a stop to the beginning and end of the main strokes of a character.”(TypeDeCon). 

Timeless

For most people, seeing a serif font reminds them of typewriters, however, the serif type style dates back to the 1500s(this isn't a research paper im not going to dig through my history for a citation). The fact that serif has remained in style for so long gives me confidence to use it as branding, and on my devices.

 

Fonts and, sustainability?

Choosing a font that has lasted the test of time is good for both your brand, and sustainable! If a large company with a physical presence ever goes through a rebrand(which happens when trends die), new signs, papers, paint, and lights could all attribute to a(while minor) use of new materials, and the old ones getting thrown out. A steady font that doesn't change will contribute to how recognised your brand identity is. Like, most readers will know what the attached image is copying, just from the font style.

My Personal experience:

I like the roboto slab font subjectively, and I think it makes using devices & reading easier. I usually wear glasses for my vision, and having a legible font when i take them off to relax my eyes helps a lot! Through some bodgy methods, I set roboto slab as the default font on my phone.

The difficulty of Samsung

 

The way I got the font to actually be accepted by the system was not the most efficient. But it is leagues better than Samsung's native offering. By default, Samsung only allows you to use fonts from the “Samsung store” as the system font; the problem comes from the sheer lack of variety in these fonts, and the fact that most of them are locked behind a paywall for seemingly no reason. I got around this using “_mono”[this is not paid endorsement]. It required me to trick samsung cloud(which is semi-blocked by verizon for some reason) into thinking that roboto slab was the default samsung font. And it left me thinking, why can’t Samsung just allow custom font installation via Good Lock at least?

 

In conclusion:

Pick a font that suits you! Look at some guides on fonts.google.com, find a free or premium font that suits you, and try not to settle for the limits that manufacturers provide you. As always, this is just the spark to inspire further reading, so look into typography if you desire! If this interests you, I'm glad I could spark interest. Thanks for reading!
